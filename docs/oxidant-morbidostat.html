Experiment Proposal · a devo application

The Oxidant Morbidostat

Run the evolution machine as an H₂O₂-morbidostat and answer a question nobody has asked: does chemical harmful-algal-bloom control select for resistant cyanobacteria?

2026-07-13 Organism: Synechocystis / Microcystis (non-toxigenic) Home: US HAB-Control Technologies Incubator
The thesis

Every chemical used to suppress a bloom is a selection pressure. The question a lake manager should ask before committing to a treatment program — does the target adapt to it? — is answered for copper and unasked for hydrogen peroxide, the leading selective alternative. This turns the evolution machine (two-instruments Part 2, best-hybrids) into an H₂O₂-morbidostat and runs the missing experiment: directed evolution of a bloom cyanobacterium under a ramped oxidant, with an automated frozen fossil record and a DuckBot assay layer. It is a devo experiment — the one purpose the field has no champion for, because no platform archives or replicates as the science requires. Here, that gap is the experiment.

Motivating context. Lake Wylie (Catawba River, NC/SC) runs an active chelated-copper algaecide program against benthic Lyngbya (Microseira wollei), and its coves have thrown toxin-producing planktonic blooms — microcystin at 8.88 µg/L, above SC's 8 µg/L recreational standard (SC DES advisory). The natural home is the US HAB-Control Technologies Incubator (NOAA / UMCES-IMET / Mote), for whom "does this control technology select for resistance?" is a first-order question. imet.usmd.edu/ushabcti

01 Three stacked novelty claims

The scoping literature is unusually clean: three separate gaps converge on one experiment.

Claim 1 · H₂O₂ resistance evolutionNever tested

Nobody has tested whether repeated H₂O₂ dosing selects for resistant cyanobacteria.

The copper contrast makes the gap sharp: heritable copper resistance in Microcystis by pre-selective spontaneous mutation is proven by Luria–Delbrück fluctuation analysis (García-Villada et al. 2004, 10.1016/j.watres.2004.01.036; ceiling, Rouco et al. 2014, 10.1007/s00442-014-2963-1). For H₂O₂, every account of reduced field efficacy invokes non-heritable mechanisms — pre-existing strain variation (Schuurmans 2018, 10.1016/j.hal.2018.07.008), plasticity of peroxiredoxin defenses (Sandrini 2020, 10.1016/j.hal.2020.101916), and community-level bacterial detox (most Microcystis lack catalase; in-situ katG is bacterial — Smith 2022, 10.1128/aem.02544-21; Weenink 2021, 10.1111/1462-2920.15429). The method exists but was never pointed at H₂O₂: the López-Rodas / Costas ratchet on Microcystis for glyphosate (Costas 2007, 10.1007/s10682-006-9134-8).

Claim 2 · Automated phototroph ALEUndemonstrated

No automated continuous-culture / morbidostat ALE has ever been run on a phototroph.

The morbidostat (Toprak 2012, 10.1038/ng.1034; low-cost build, Liu 2016, 10.3791/54426), eVOLVER (10.1038/nbt.4151), and Chi.Bio (10.1371/journal.pbio.3000794) were all demonstrated on heterotrophs. The microalgal-ALE review states algal evolution is done by manual serial transfer, the automated paradigm "not adopted" (Zhang 2021, 10.3389/fmicb.2021.737248). The nearest prior art is concept-only — the "Photoinhibistat," a control proposal with no evolutionary run (Mairet & Bernard 2016, 10.1016/j.ifacol.2016.07.344). A morbidostat ramping a chemical stressor on a phototroph, with sequencing, would be a first.

Claim 3 · Automated phototroph fossil recordWhite space

Automated cryo-archiving of a phototroph fossil record has never been assembled.

The design is established — replicate lineages plus a revived-ancestor common-garden competition (Collins & Bell 2004, 10.1038/nature02945; Lohbeck 2012, 10.1038/ngeo1441), the descendant of the LTEE frozen record (Lenski 1991, 10.1086/285289). But the engineering — automated fraction-collection + cryo-archiving in an algal ALE loop — has never been built; the one representative automated phototroph ALE (Barten 2022, 10.1002/biot.202100659) kept archiving manual and offline. This is exactly the automated chilled fraction collector proposed as the evolution machine's one new build.

03 Design and rigor

  • Organism (biosafety-first). Selection on the non-toxigenic Synechocystis sp. PCC 6803 or the Microcystis aeruginosa PCC 7806 ΔmcyB mutant (Dittmann 1997, 10.1046/j.1365-2958.1997.6131982.x). Microcystis is BSL-1; microcystin is not a federal Select Agent (saxitoxin is). Escalate to a toxigenic strain only under permit and containment.
  • The morbidostat law. A feedback loop reads growth from near-IR OD each cycle and doses H₂O₂ to hold inhibition at a set fraction of the untreated rate — ramping the effective dose upward exactly as, and only as, the population adapts. Selectivity holds below ~5 mg/L and erodes at high biomass (Weenink 2015, 10.3389/fmicb.2015.00714).
  • Separate pre-existing from evolved — the load-bearing control. Interleave Luria–Delbrück fluctuation analysis so a tolerance rise is attributed to selection of standing variants versus induced plasticity. Without it the result is not interpretable — the exact conflation the H₂O₂ literature has never resolved.
  • Revive-and-compete. From the automated archive, revive ancestor and descendant and run a common-garden H₂O₂ dose-response (the DuckBot assay) to quantify the resistance gain — the Lohbeck design, made automatic.
  • Read the genome. Whole-genome resequencing of endpoint clones and convergent timepoints; look for oxidative-stress loci (katG/catalase, peroxiredoxin prx, thioredoxin, perR/fur). Convergence across replicate lineages is the causal signal.
  • Watch the toxin. Any H₂O₂ dose-response releases intracellular microcystin on lysis; measure dissolved toxin, not just cell death, on any toxigenic run.

04 Prior art to differentiate against

  • Photoinhibistat (Mairet & Bernard 2016) — morbidostat logic with light, concept only, no evolution or genomics. The clearest thing to cite and differ from.
  • Copper resistance selection (García-Villada 2004) — the method template and the proof the concern is real; H₂O₂ is the untested counterpart.
  • Cyanobacterial ALE is mature — thermotolerance (Tillich 2014, 10.1186/1472-6750-14-66, the one precedent in an automated turbidostat-mode PBR), high light (Yoshikawa 2021, 10.1038/s42003-021-01875-y), cadmium (Xu 2018, 10.1186/s13068-018-1205-x). The biology is de-risked; the automation and the stressor are new.
  • Barten 2022 — automated Picochlorum turbidostat with manual archiving; the automated fossil record is what this adds.

05 What this is not, and the honest limits

  • Duckweed is not the devo arm. Lemna reproduces clonally (~3–4 day doubling, rare sex), so the duckweed work is accession screening / clonal selection, not mutation-accumulation ALE. DuckBot's value there is phenotyping and dose-response assays, not evolving the organism.
  • ALPHA correction. The Sartor "heat + ammonia tolerance" selection paper sometimes assumed in the duckweed line does not exist: ALPHA (Robinson 2025, Plant Direct, 10.1002/pld3.70048) is a salt-tolerance phenotyping platform; heat/ammonia are breeding goals in press coverage only.
  • Interpretability ceiling. A community-lab result is a screen, not a regulatory arbiter: lab selection can over- or under-state field evolvability, and single-strain runs miss the community detox that dominates the field. The claim is "resistance is/isn't readily selectable under controlled oxidant ramping" — decision-relevant precisely because it is currently unknown either way.

If H₂O₂ resistance evolves readily, a peroxide bloom-control program is a treadmill and the money should go to nutrient control; if it does not, H₂O₂ is a more durable tool than copper. Either answer is a first-of-kind result — produced by the one machine the field has been missing: a devo instrument that archives and replicates.
